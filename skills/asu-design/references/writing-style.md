# ASU Writing Style & Content
> Source: ASU Unity Design System (UDS) — Brand Guide
> Load this file when writing UI copy, headlines, CTAs, body content, labels, or any text that will appear in an ASU-branded interface.

---

## Brand Voice

The ASU brand voice is: **ambitious, bold, visionary, inspiring, aspirational, optimistic, determined, future-focused, authoritative, leading the way, strong, active, capable, committed, purposeful, honest.**

ASU language is direct, clear and sincere. It is not overly clever, catchy, slick or cute.

### Voice Qualities

- **Genuine**, not slick
- **Down to earth**, not excessively idealistic
- **Smart**, not clever or satirical
- **Heartfelt and caring**, not overly sentimental
- **Optimistic**, not dreamy
- **Bold**, not weak

### Avoid

- Abbreviations, acronyms not readily recognizable by the reader
- Jargon when communicating to general audiences
- Long headings/titles — five words is a good target
- Poor grammar (person/tense switching)
- Unnecessary phrases — substitute or rewrite
- University size and enrollment numbers
- Creating ambiguity with symbols (and/or, he/she)

### Cost Language

- **Never mention cost or position ASU programs as free** — weakens the brand
- **Never mention discounts** but acceptable to advertise scholarships (e.g., "Get a $5,000 scholarship if you apply by July 1")
- Scholarships are recommended as supporting copy, not headlines

### Inclusion

All materials should reflect inclusion, equity and belonging (based on ASU Charter). Use gender-neutral language, people-first disability language.

## Capitalization

### Headlines
- Sentence case — first letter capitalized only
- **Exception:** First word after a colon is always capitalized
- ✅ "ASU journalism program recognized by the New York Times"
- ❌ "Engineering Degree Programs" (don't capitalize common nouns)

### Menus and Navigation
- **Title case** — first letter of each word capitalized (for accessibility optimization)
- ✅ "About Us"
- ❌ "About us"

### Body Copy After a Colon
- Capitalize only if it starts a complete sentence

### Forms
- Capitalize full names of forms/applications. Lowercase for descriptive use.
- ✅ "ASU Scholarship Estimator" / "the scholarship estimator"

### URLs
- Always lowercase: asu.edu, amazon.com

### All-Caps Rule
**Never use all-caps for any text in UI.** This is absolute — no exceptions for eyebrow labels, context labels, or section category markers. Never apply `uppercase` (or use `tracking-wider` as a shortcut to fake small-caps). Use sentence case at the appropriate type size instead.

## Formatting

### Text Styles
- **Bold:** Use sparingly. Never bold entire paragraphs.
- **Underline:** Only to indicate a link. Never decoratively.
- **Italics:** Do not use. Never permitted under any circumstances.
- **Subscript/Superscript:** Avoid. Use same text type as numeral (✅ "1st" ❌ "1ˢᵗ"). Exception: trademark symbols are always superscript.

### Headlines Formatting
- Sentence case always
- Use figures for all numerals, including those less than 10 (✅ "5 ASU students win...")
- Do not use periods/dots at end, even after a complete sentence

### Links and URLs in Copy
- ✅ "asu.edu" — ❌ "ASU.edu" or "https://www.asu.edu"
- Never use "here," "go," or "click" to link. Use descriptive keywords.
- ✅ "Learn more about ASU web standards"
- ❌ "To learn more about web standards, click here"

### Phone Numbers
- Format: 480-965-1234 (hyphens, not dots or parentheses)

### Photo Captions
- Present tense for the action in the photo
- Supply information the picture cannot

## Times, Dates, Months

### Times
- Lowercase with periods: 8 a.m., 10:15 p.m. (Exception: noon)
- No specs for top of hour: ✅ "7 p.m." ❌ "7:00 p.m."
- Spans in text use "between": ✅ "between 8 a.m. and 5 p.m."

### Dates
- Do not use numerical dates in headlines: ✅ "Election day is Tuesday, Nov. 5" ❌ "11/5/18"
- In grids/forms: forward slash separator (8/17/18)

### Months
- Abbreviate: Jan., Feb., Aug., Sept., Oct., Nov., Dec.
- Spell out: March, April, May, June, July (even with a day)
- No ordinal suffixes: ✅ "Aug. 12, 1975" ❌ "Aug. 12th, 1975"

### Seasons
- Lowercase unless beginning a sentence or part of a formal name

### Years
- Academic years: hyphen (2023–24)
- Decades: numeral with lowercase s, no apostrophe (the 1960s)

---

## Sentences & Punctuation

- Use serial (Oxford) comma: red, white, and blue
- One space after periods
- Em dash (—) with no spaces on either side
- Ellipsis: three periods no spaces (...) — avoid in formal copy

### Lists
- Sentence fragments when possible
- Capitalize first word of each bullet
- No periods unless bullets are complete sentences
- If one bullet is a complete sentence, all should be
